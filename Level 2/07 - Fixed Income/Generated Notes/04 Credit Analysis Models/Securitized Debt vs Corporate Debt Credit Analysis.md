# Securitized Debt vs Corporate Debt Credit Analysis

## Core Difference

Corporate debt analysis focuses on the issuer's whole business.

Securitized debt analysis focuses on a pool of specific assets and the structure that redirects their cash flows.

```text
Corporate bond:
Can the company pay?

Securitized bond:
Will the collateral pool generate enough cash,
and will the structure allocate that cash to this tranche?
```

## Corporate Debt Analysis

Corporate credit analysis emphasizes:

| Area | Question |
|---|---|
| Business risk | How stable are revenue and margins? |
| Financial risk | How much leverage and interest burden exists? |
| Cash flow | Can operating cash flow service debt? |
| Management and strategy | Are capital allocation and governance sound? |
| Seniority/security | Where does the bond sit in the capital structure? |
| Recovery | What can creditors recover if default occurs? |

The corporate bond is a claim on the issuer's general creditworthiness.

## Securitized Debt Analysis

Securitized debt is backed by a collateral pool, often held by a bankruptcy-remote special purpose entity.

Credit analysis emphasizes:

| Area | Question |
|---|---|
| Collateral quality | What assets generate the cash flows? |
| Pool diversification | Are defaults correlated or diversified? |
| Homogeneity | Are loans similar enough for statistical pool analysis? |
| Granularity | Are there many small loans or a few large concentrated loans? |
| Structure/waterfall | Which tranche gets paid first? |
| Credit enhancement | What subordination, overcollateralization, reserve accounts, or guarantees exist? |
| Servicer quality | Can the servicer collect and manage cash flows effectively? |
| Legal structure | Are assets isolated from issuer bankruptcy? |
| Prepayment/extension risk | How can timing of cash flows change? |

## Why Bankruptcy Remoteness Matters

```text
Originator sells assets to SPE
  |
  v
SPE issues securities backed by assets
  |
  v
If originator fails, collateral should remain isolated
```

This can lower financing costs and allow investors to analyze the collateral separately from the originator's entire balance sheet.

## Benefits And Costs

| Party | Benefit | Cost / Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Issuer/originator | Lower funding cost, balance sheet flexibility | Complexity and structuring costs |
| Investor | Diversification, potentially higher spread for rating | Complexity, model risk, servicer risk, collateral uncertainty |

## Homogeneity And Granularity

| Pool Feature | Meaning | Analytical Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Homogeneous pool | Loans are similar | Statistical pool analysis is more useful |
| Heterogeneous pool | Loans differ meaningfully | Loan-by-loan analysis becomes more important |
| Granular pool | Many small obligations | Idiosyncratic risk is more diversified |
| Non-granular pool | Few large obligations | Individual borrower analysis matters more |

## Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|---|---|
| Analyzing securitized debt like a normal corporate bond | Focus on collateral, structure, and servicer risk. |
| Ignoring tranche priority | Same collateral pool can create very different risks by tranche. |
| Treating rating as enough | Complexity and model assumptions matter. |
| Forgetting bankruptcy remoteness | SPE isolation is central to securitization credit analysis. |
| Ignoring prepayment/extension risk | Timing of cash flows can materially affect value and risk. |
| Treating all securitized pools statistically | Heterogeneous or non-granular pools may require loan-level analysis. |

## Memory Hook

```text
Corporate credit = company pays.
Securitized credit = collateral pays, structure allocates.
```
