# Mean Reversion in Earnings and Accruals

Mean reversion in earnings means unusually high or unusually low profitability tends to move back toward a normal level over time. For CFA Level II, the key refinement is that **earnings backed by cash flows persist longer than earnings driven by accruals**.

The exam often gives two companies with similar current earnings and asks which earnings stream is higher quality. The answer depends on sustainability, not just size.

## Why Earnings Mean-Revert

Extreme earnings attract economic forces that push them back toward normal.

If earnings are abnormally high:

1. Competitors notice high profitability.
2. New entrants or existing rivals increase supply.
3. Pricing power falls.
4. Margins and returns decline toward normal.

If earnings are abnormally low:

1. Management cuts costs or exits weak segments.
2. Competitors may leave the market.
3. Capacity tightens.
4. Profitability can recover.

Mean reversion is not automatic every quarter, but it is a strong long-run force in competitive markets.

## Sustainable Earnings

[[Sustainable earnings]] are earnings expected to persist into the future and provide an adequate return on invested capital.

High-quality earnings are:

- Recurring.
- Cash-backed.
- Generated from core operations.
- Not dependent on one-time gains.
- Not driven by aggressive accounting estimates.
- Sufficient to cover the cost of capital.

Low-quality earnings may be high today but hard to forecast because they are nonrecurring, accrual-heavy, or manipulated.

## Cash vs Accrual Components

Earnings can be decomposed into:

$$
\text{Earnings} = \text{Cash flow component} + \text{Accrual component}
$$

Research and CFA curriculum logic emphasize that cash flow components are usually more persistent than accrual components.

A simplified persistence model:

$$
Earnings_{t+1} =
\alpha + \beta_1(CashFlow_t) + \beta_2(Accruals_t) + \epsilon
$$

The key relationship:

$$
\beta_1 > \beta_2
$$

This means one dollar of cash-flow-backed earnings is expected to persist more than one dollar of accrual-backed earnings.

## Why Accruals Mean-Revert Faster

Accruals rely on estimates and timing assumptions.

Examples:

- Revenue recognized before cash collection.
- Bad debt estimates.
- Warranty reserves.
- Inventory write-down assumptions.
- Depreciation estimates.
- Capitalization vs expensing choices.

Accruals eventually reverse. If a company recognizes revenue aggressively today, cash must eventually show up. If it does not, future earnings or cash flows will disappoint.

## Discretionary vs Nondiscretionary Accruals

| Accrual type | Meaning | Earnings-quality implication |
|---|---|---|
| Nondiscretionary accruals | Normal accruals from ordinary business activity | Not automatically a red flag |
| Discretionary accruals | Accruals driven by managerial judgment beyond normal operations | Greater manipulation risk |

Growing companies naturally have accruals. For example, credit sales create accounts receivable. The concern is abnormal accruals that do not match the company's business model or cash flow pattern.

## Worked Example

Firm A and Firm B both report ROE of 25%. The industry normalized ROE is 12%.

| Company | Current ROE | Cash-flow component | Accrual component |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Firm A | 25% | 90% | 10% |
| Firm B | 25% | 20% | 80% |

Both firms have high current profitability, so both may eventually mean-revert. But Firm A's earnings are more cash-backed, so the high ROE is more likely to persist. Firm B's earnings are accrual-heavy, so an analyst should assume faster mean reversion.

In valuation:

- Firm A may justify a slower fade of abnormal ROE.
- Firm B should receive a faster fade rate or lower normalized earnings estimate.

## Valuation Implications

Do not blindly extrapolate peak earnings.

For valuation:

| Situation | Analyst response |
|---|---|
| High cash-backed earnings | More confidence in persistence |
| High accrual-backed earnings | Faster fade or normalization |
| One-time gains | Remove from normalized earnings |
| Losses from temporary shock | Consider recovery toward mid-cycle earnings |
| High discretionary accruals | Increase skepticism and required margin of safety |

This is especially important in residual income valuation, where abnormal ROE persistence drives value.

## Exam Traps

- **High earnings are not automatically high quality.** Ask whether they are sustainable.
- **Accruals are not automatically manipulation.** Normal business growth creates nondiscretionary accruals.
- **Discretionary accruals are more concerning.** They depend more on management judgment.
- **Cash-flow-backed earnings persist longer.**
- **Extreme earnings should usually be normalized.** Do not extrapolate peak or trough margins forever.
- **Accrual-heavy earnings mean-revert faster.** Use a faster fade rate in forecasts.

## Memory Hook

**Cash earnings stick. Accrual earnings slip.**

When two companies report the same profit, trust the one whose cash flows tell the same story.
