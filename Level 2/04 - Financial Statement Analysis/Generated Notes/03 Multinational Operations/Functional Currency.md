# Functional Currency

Functional currency is the currency of the primary economic environment in which an entity generates and spends cash. It is the currency that best reflects the economics of the subsidiary, not necessarily the currency printed on the local banknotes.

This is the most important decision in multinational operations because it determines the translation method, where translation gains and losses go, and how ratios behave.

## Three Currencies

| Currency | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Local currency | Currency of the country where the subsidiary operates | Mexican peso for a Mexican subsidiary |
| Functional currency | Currency of the subsidiary's primary economic environment | USD if the subsidiary sells oil in global USD markets |
| Presentation currency | Currency used by the parent in consolidated statements | USD for a US parent |

The local currency and functional currency often match, but they do not have to.

## Decision Rule

The central question is:

> Which currency drives the subsidiary's sales prices, costs, financing, and cash flows?

| Factor | Points toward local currency | Points toward parent's currency |
|---|---|---|
| Sales prices | Local supply and demand determine prices | Prices are set by parent currency or global markets |
| Sales market | Sales mainly local | Sales mainly to parent or export markets |
| Costs | Labor and materials sourced locally | Major inputs sourced from parent country |
| Financing | Borrowing and capital raised locally | Parent funds the subsidiary |
| Cash flows | Cash retained and reinvested locally | Cash regularly remitted to parent |

When the indicators are mixed, management uses judgment. That judgment creates a comparability problem: two economically similar companies can report different net income, assets, and ratios because they chose different functional currencies.

## Method Selection

Functional currency drives the method.

| Functional currency relationship | Method | Translation gain/loss location |
|---|---|---|
| Functional currency differs from parent's presentation currency | [[Current Rate Method]] | [[Other comprehensive income]] through CTA |
| Functional currency equals parent's presentation currency | [[Temporal Method]] | Income statement |

The logic is:

- If the subsidiary really operates in its own foreign currency, translation is mainly a parent-reporting exercise. The gain/loss goes to equity through [[Cumulative translation adjustment]].
- If the subsidiary really operates in the parent's currency, foreign-currency balances are remeasured as if the subsidiary had been keeping books in the parent's currency all along. The gain/loss affects net income.

## Example

A US parent owns two foreign subsidiaries.

| Subsidiary | Facts | Likely functional currency | Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Local retailer | Sells locally, pays local wages, borrows locally | Local currency | Current rate |
| Export commodity producer | Sells in USD, buys major inputs in USD, remits cash to parent | USD | Temporal |

The same legal structure can produce different accounting outcomes because the economic currency differs.

## Why Analysts Care

Functional currency affects:

- Whether currency effects bypass net income or hit net income.
- Whether balance sheet exposure is net assets or net monetary assets.
- Whether inventory and fixed assets are translated at current or historical rates.
- Whether mixed ratios such as fixed asset turnover are distorted.
- Whether comparisons across firms require adjustment.

## Exam Traps

| Trap | Correct interpretation |
|---|---|
| "Functional currency is always the local currency." | False. It depends on the primary economic environment. |
| "Presentation currency determines the method." | Not by itself. The functional currency relative to presentation currency determines the method. |
| "Current rate and temporal differ only in exchange rates used." | They also differ in where translation effects are reported. |
| "Management choice is irrelevant." | Functional currency involves judgment and can materially affect comparability. |

## Memory Hook

Functional currency answers: "What currency does this business really live in?"

Presentation currency answers: "What currency does the parent report in?"
