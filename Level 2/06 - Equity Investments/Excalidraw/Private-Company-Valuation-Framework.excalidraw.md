# Private Company Valuation Framework

# Excalidraw Data

## Text Elements
Private Company Valuation Framework ^t_txt

STEP 1
Normalize Earnings
(Fix the Numerator) ^BXYtYmw0

Adjust for:
• Owner salary vs. market salary
• Non-arm's-length transactions
• One-time / non-recurring items
• Tax minimization distortions
• Non-operating assets ^oZIf4IF8

→ Get true,
  recurring earnings ^ApWUsK1a

STEP 2
Adjust Discount Rate
(Fix the Denominator) ^LJvDzshL

Add to discount rate for:
• Size premium (small co. = more risk)
• Specific company risk premium
• Illiquidity of private shares
• Key person risk
• Customer concentration risk ^KgmKlf0y

→ r for private co.
  always > public comp ^F8ycGvd9

STEP 3
Apply Premiums
& Discounts
(Final Adjustments) ^tRI7yCNa

Discounts:
• DLOC: Discount for lack of control
• DLOM: Discount for lack of marketability

Premiums:
• Control premium (for majority stake)
• Strategic premium (synergies) ^qzeTLJ6a

→ Adjust final
  value up or down ^xaeZtTKK

Three Valuation Approaches:   Income Approach (DCF/RI)   |   Market Approach (Comparables/Multiples)   |   Asset-Based Approach ^mth_t

Income Approach (DCF) ^m_0_hdr

PV of future cash flows or residual income.
Best for: going-concern firms with forecastable cash flows. ^m_0_det

Market Approach ^NeYheAAh

Guideline public company method or transaction multiples.
Best for: when comparable firms or deals exist. ^IhwxRNHy

Asset-Based Approach ^Na6g3Awj

Adjusted book value of net assets.
Best for: holding companies, early-stage, or near-liquidation firms. ^CoCq0WGI
