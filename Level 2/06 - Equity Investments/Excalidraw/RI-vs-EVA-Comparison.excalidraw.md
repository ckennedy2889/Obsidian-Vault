# RI vs EVA Comparison

# Excalidraw Data

## Text Elements
Residual Income (RI) vs. Economic Value Added (EVA) ^t_txt

Both measure ECONOMIC PROFIT: profit earned ABOVE and BEYOND the cost of the capital used to generate it. ^core_t

Residual Income (RI) ^ri_hdr_t

Formula: ^ri_r0_l

RI = Net Income − (r × B_{t-1}) ^ri_r0_v

Profit measure: ^ri_r1_l

Net Income (after interest) ^ri_r1_v

Capital charge: ^ri_r2_l

Cost of Equity (r) × Beginning Book Value ^ri_r2_v

Perspective: ^ri_r3_l

Equity holders only ^ri_r3_v

r used: ^ri_r4_l

Cost of Equity (ke) ^ri_r4_v

Capital base: ^ri_r5_l

Book Value of Equity (B) ^ri_r5_v

Sign: ^ri_r6_l

Positive RI = value creation above equity cost ^ri_r6_v

Economic Value Added (EVA) ^YsbC44jz

Formula: ^eva_r0_l

EVA = NOPAT − (WACC × TC) ^eva_r0_v

Profit measure: ^eva_r1_l

NOPAT (before interest deduction) ^eva_r1_v

Capital charge: ^eva_r2_l

WACC × Total Capital (debt + equity) ^eva_r2_v

Perspective: ^eva_r3_l

ALL capital providers (debt + equity) ^eva_r3_v

r used: ^eva_r4_l

WACC (blended debt + equity cost) ^eva_r4_v

Capital base: ^eva_r5_l

Total Invested Capital (TC) ^eva_r5_v

Sign: ^eva_r6_l

Positive EVA = value above total capital cost ^eva_r6_v

VS ^vs_t

MVA (Market Value Added) = PV of all future EVAs = Market Value − Book Value of Total Capital
MVA > 0 means the market expects the company to continue creating economic value ^mva_t
